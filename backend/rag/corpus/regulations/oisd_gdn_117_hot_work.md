# OISD-GDN-117: Safety Requirements for Hot Work Operations

Framework: OISD
Clause: OISD-GDN-117 Section 4.2

## Scope
This guideline covers safety requirements for hot work operations including welding, cutting, grinding, and other operations producing sparks or heat in oil, gas, and industrial facilities.

## Key Requirements

### Gas Monitoring (Section 4.2)
Hot work shall not be permitted within 15 meters of any area where gas monitoring alarms are active. Continuous gas monitoring must be maintained during all hot work operations. Gas-free certificates must be obtained for adjacent confined spaces before commencing hot work.

### Permit Requirements (Section 5.1)
All hot work requires a valid permit-to-work issued by authorized personnel. The permit must specify: work location, duration, fire watch assignment, gas monitoring plan, and emergency procedures. Permits exceeding 12 hours require re-authorization.

### Simultaneous Operations (Section 6.3)
Hot work shall not be conducted simultaneously with lifting operations, confined space entry, or excavation in the same zone. SIMOPS assessments are mandatory when multiple high-risk activities overlap.

## Historical Pattern
Gas ignition during hot work has been identified as a precursor pattern in multiple industrial incidents. The combination of elevated gas readings with active hot work permits in proximity represents a critical compound risk factor.
