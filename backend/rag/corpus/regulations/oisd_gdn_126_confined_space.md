# OISD-GDN-126: Confined Space Entry Safety

Framework: OISD
Clause: OISD-GDN-126 Section 3.3

## Scope
Guidelines for safe entry into confined spaces in oil and gas installations and industrial facilities.

## Key Requirements

### Gas Monitoring (Section 3.3)
Continuous gas monitoring is mandatory at all confined space entry points. Entry is prohibited when H2S levels exceed 10 ppm or LEL exceeds 10%. Monitors must be calibrated within 24 hours of use.

### Ventilation (Section 4.1)
Adequate ventilation must be confirmed operational before entry. Forced ventilation systems must be tested and verified. Entry is prohibited if ventilation systems are offline or under maintenance.

### Entry Permit (Section 2.2)
Confined space entry permits require: atmospheric testing results, ventilation confirmation, rescue plan, attendant assignment, and communication protocol.

## Incident Pattern
H2S exposure in confined spaces remains one of the leading causes of industrial fatalities. The pattern of H2S drift into entry zones during active permits, especially during shift changeover periods, has been documented in multiple near-miss investigations.
